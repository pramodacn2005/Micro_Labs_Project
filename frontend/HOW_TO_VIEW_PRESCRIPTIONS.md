# How to View Your Prescriptions (Patient Guide)

## Where to Find Your Prescriptions

After a doctor sends you a prescription, you can view it in the **"My Prescriptions"** section.

## Steps to View Prescriptions

### 1. Navigate to Prescriptions
- Open the **Sidebar** (left side of the screen)
- Look for **"My Prescriptions"** (ðŸ“‹ icon)
- Click on it

### 2. View Your Prescriptions
- All prescriptions sent by doctors will be listed here
- Each prescription shows:
  - **Doctor Name** and License Number
  - **Clinic Information** (name and address)
  - **Issue Date** and Time
  - **Medications List** (with dosage and frequency)
  - **Notes** from the doctor
  - **Follow-up Date** (if provided)
  - **Refill Status** (Allowed or Not Allowed)
  - **Sent Status** (Sent, Failed, Pending, etc.)

### 3. Download Prescription PDF
- Click the **"Download PDF"** button on any prescription
- The PDF will be saved to your device
- The PDF contains all prescription details in a professional format

### 4. Refresh Prescriptions
- Click the **"Refresh"** button (top right) to reload prescriptions
- Use this if you just received a new prescription

## Troubleshooting

### "No Prescriptions" Message
- Make sure your doctor has sent you a prescription
- Check if you're logged in with the correct account
- Click "Refresh" to reload prescriptions
- Contact your doctor if you expected a prescription

### Can't Download PDF
- Check your internet connection
- Try refreshing the page
- Contact support if the issue persists

### Prescription Not Showing
- Wait a few moments and click "Refresh"
- Make sure the prescription was sent to your account
- Verify you're viewing the correct patient account

## Notes

- Prescriptions are stored securely
- You can download PDFs anytime
- All prescriptions are saved in your account
- Prescriptions are sorted by date (newest first)

## Support

If you have issues viewing prescriptions:
1. Check that you're logged in
2. Verify your account matches the one the doctor used
3. Try refreshing the page
4. Contact your doctor or system administrator
